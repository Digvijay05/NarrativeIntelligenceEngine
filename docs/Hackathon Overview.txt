ET Gen AI Hackathon Overview

1. Overview
The ET Gen AI Hackathon, in partnership with Unstop, is a nation-scale innovation challenge aimed at identifying and celebrating India's emerging talent in Generative AI. The competition brings together students, professionals, and AI enthusiasts to solve real-world GenAI problems spanning business innovation, social impact, and open innovation.
The hackathon follows a multi-phase journey:
* Online GenAI assessment
* Idea submission
* Prototype and MVP development
* Offline Grand Finale
Participants receive expert mentorship, structured evaluations, and national visibility, with the objective of accelerating India's GenAI ecosystem and enabling participants to translate ideas into impactful AI solutions.

2. Eligibility Criteria
* Open to participants across India (students, professionals, AI enthusiasts).
* Team size: 1 to 4 members.
* Cross-college, cross-discipline, and mixed-background teams are allowed.
* Each participant may join only one team.
* Intended for innovators, designers, developers, and problem-solvers interested in building real GenAI solutions.

3. Rules and Guidelines
* Team formation must be completed within the registration window.
* No changes to team members are allowed after the deadline.
* All ideas, code, documents, and assets must be:
o Original
o Created during the hackathon
* Plagiarism, pre-built projects, or unauthorised third-party material will result in immediate disqualification.
* Use of open-source tools, libraries, and models is permitted.
* Licensed tools or datasets may be used only with valid authorisation.
* All decisions regarding eligibility, authenticity, and judgment rest with Unstop and The Economic Times.

4. Judging Criteria
Submissions will be evaluated based on:
* Innovation & Creativity
* Technical Implementation
* Feasibility & Scalability
* Relevance to the Problem Statement
* Documentation & Presentation Quality

5. Final Authority
* Jury decisions are final and binding.
* Organizers reserve the right to modify rules, guidelines, or timelines if required.

6. Rewards & Prizes
* Winner (Grand Finale): INR 5,00,000
* First Runners-Up: INR 3,00,000
* Second Runners-Up: INR 2,00,000
Additional benefits:
* PPIs, internships, and job opportunities with leading AI-first tech companies.
* Certificate of Participation for all participants (issued by The Economic Times).
* Certificate of Merit for top 3 teams per college.
* National-level recognition for finalists and winners across ET platforms and media.
* Networking opportunities with experts, founders, and AI practitioners.

7. Stages and Timelines
Phase 1: Online Qualifier - MCQ
Duration:
* 26 Dec 2025, 12:00 PM IST → 27 Jan 2026, 11:59 PM IST
Details:
* Participants attempt a GenAI-focused MCQ assessment.
* Immediately after the assessment, participants submit an initial idea aligned with the problem themes.
* Shortlisting is based on:
o Assessment scores
o Relevance and clarity of the idea
Note:
* This round can only be attempted on a desktop or laptop.

Phase 1: Online Qualifier - Idea Submission
Duration:
* 26 Dec 2025, 01:20 PM IST → 01 Feb 2026, 11:59 PM IST
Details:
* Submission of the initial idea after completing the MCQ assessment.
* Evaluation based on:
o Clarity
o Relevance
o Potential of the idea
* Used for shortlisting teams to the next stage.

Phase 2: Prototype Submission
Duration:
* 07 Feb 2026, 06:00 PM IST → 15 Feb 2026, 11:59 PM IST
Details:
* Teams build a functional prototype.
* Submission must include:
o Architecture
o Code
o Demo video
* Evaluation based on:
o Innovation
o Execution quality
o Impact

Phase 3: MVP Build Sprint
Duration:
* 23 Feb 2026, 12:00 PM IST → 04 Mar 2026, 06:00 PM IST
Details:
* Top 50 teams are shortlisted.
* Teams refine ideas and develop a Minimum Viable Product (MVP).

Phase 4: Grand Finale
Date: To be announced
Details:
* Top 10 teams present their ideas and demonstrate prototypes before the Jury Panel.
* Final winners are selected in this stage.
